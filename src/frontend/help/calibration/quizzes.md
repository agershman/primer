Calibration quizzes are Primer's most accurate tool for measuring your understanding. Unlike multiple-choice tests or keyword matching, Primer uses open-ended questions that require you to articulate your knowledge in your own words.

## One Per Briefing

Each daily briefing includes one calibration quiz question, targeting the concept where better calibration would be most valuable. Primer prioritizes:

1. **Low-confidence concepts** — Where the depth score is uncertain
2. **Recently decayed concepts** — Where the score may no longer be accurate
3. **Newly extracted concepts** — Where no calibration data exists yet

## Question Design

Quiz questions are designed to **probe understanding, not memorization**. You won't be asked to define a term or recall a fact. Instead, you'll face questions that require you to:

- Explain *why* something works the way it does
- Describe *when* you'd choose one approach over another
- Identify *what would happen if* a configuration changed
- Articulate *how* you'd troubleshoot a specific failure

The question is contextualized to your work — if the concept relates to a Linear issue you're working on, the question may reference that specific situation.

## Answering

Write 2–4 sentences. You don't need to write an essay, but "it's a thing that does stuff" won't give Primer enough signal. Aim for the level of explanation you'd give a smart colleague who asked you about the topic.

### Speaking instead of typing (voice mode)

The microphone button in the top-right of every answer textarea puts you into **voice mode** — tap to start, talk freely (with pauses to think), tap again when you're done. **`Esc`** also stops the mic instantly without dismissing the quiz panel. The transcript appears live as you speak. Voice mode is purpose-built for stream-of-consciousness answers: pauses don't terminate the session, the recognizer auto-restarts in the background, and only walking away (~5 seconds of silence) ends it. See [Baseline Calibration → Speaking your answer](/help/calibration/baseline) for the full behavior — same component on both surfaces.

You can also **skip** a quiz if you don't want to answer right now. Skipped quizzes don't affect your depth score, but the concept remains a high-priority calibration target for the next briefing.

## What Happens After

Your answer is assessed asynchronously and you'll see the results in the assessment panel. See [Quiz Assessment](/help/calibration/assessment) for details on how your answer is evaluated and what the output looks like.
